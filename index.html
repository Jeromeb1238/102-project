    Featured Receipe

    This period we list an old German receipe. This recipe is one of the easiest for German bread experience. It uses half white, half whole wheat, sourdough, and caraway seeds. Two rises make this bread light and easy to slice. It's perfect for serving with soup or braut. The recipe will produce 2 full size loaves 4x8" pan.

    Ingredients

    • 2 1/2 cups of white flour
    • 2 1/2 cups whole wheat flour
    • 1/2 cup rolled oats
    • 2 tsp salt
    • 2 tsp caraway seed
    • 1 1/2 tsp instant yeast
    • 1 cup milk
    • 1 tbls vinegar
    • 1 cup plain yogurt
    • 1 to 2 tbls water
    • 1/4 cup sourdough culture

    Steps to Making Bread

    1. Gather ingredients
    2. Mix flours, oats, salt, caraway and instant yeast in large bowl
    3. Mix milk and vinegar in small bowl
    4. Add sour milk, yogurt, and sourdough culture and begin mixing.
    5. Mix until dough forms a ball; should be slightly sticky
    6. Continue kneeding 5-7 minutes on lightly floured board
    7. Let dough stand 5 minutes
    8. Form into smooth ball and place in oiled bowl
    9. Cover and let rise in warm place
    10. After rise double in size, punch down, kneed, and form into 2 loaves
    11. Put in bread pan, put in warm place, let rise until double
    12. Preheat oven to 450 and bake 20 minutes
    13. Turn oven down and continue baking 20-30 minutes until loaf brown
    14. Remove, let cool 2 hrs, and enjoy
